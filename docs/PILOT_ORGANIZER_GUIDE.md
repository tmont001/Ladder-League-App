# Ladder League — Pilot Organizer Guide

**Audience:** League organizers running the first pilot.
**Production URL:** `https://ladder-league-app.vercel.app` ← replace before sharing

---

## 1. Signing In as an Organizer

Organizers sign in with a **Magic Link** — a one-time email link. No password needed.

1. Open the production URL in your browser.
2. Click **Sign in as organizer**.
3. Enter your email address and click **Send link**.
4. Check your inbox (and spam folder) for an email from the app.
5. Click the link in that email. You will be signed in automatically.
6. The link is single-use and expires after a short time. If it has expired, repeat the steps to request a new one.
7. Your session remains active in that browser tab until you close it or your session expires. If the app shows the sign-in screen again, simply request another Magic Link.

---

## 2. Your Dashboard

After signing in you will see **My Leagues** — a list of every league you have created.

- Each league card shows the mode (Round Robin or Ladder), player count, status, and quick actions.
- Click **Open** to enter a league's dashboard.
- Use the top-right button to switch back to **My Leagues** from any league.

---

## 3. Understanding the Two League Modes

|                      | Round Robin                               | Ladder                                 |
| -------------------- | ----------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| How matches are made | A full schedule is generated at launch    | Players issue challenges to each other |
| Rounds               | Configurable (2–16)                       | Not applicable — no pre-set schedule   |
| Standings            | By wins/sets/games                        | By current ladder position             |
| Best for             | Short group seasons with a fixed schedule | Ongoing ranked competition             |

---

## 4. Creating a Round Robin League

1. From **My Leagues**, click **New League**.
2. **Step 1 — Configure:**
   - Enter a league name.
   - Choose your sport (Tennis or Pickleball).
   - Select **Singles** or **Doubles**.
   - Select **Round Robin** as the mode.
   - Set your match format (Best of 1, 3, or 5).
   - Set the number of rounds (2–16).
   - Set the match time limit.
   - Toggle **Auto-advance rounds** on or off.
   - Click **Next**.
3. **Step 2 — Add Players (or Teams):**
   - Click **Add Player** and fill in name and rating (optional).
   - For Doubles, click **Add Team** and assign two players to each team.
   - Add all participants before launching.
   - Review the settings summary on the right. You can adjust rounds here before launch.
   - Round 1 matchups are previewed on the right when enough players are added.
   - When ready, click **Launch League**.
4. Confirm the launch in the dialog that appears.
5. The **Player Codes** screen appears. Copy and share each code securely (see Section 7).

---

## 5. Creating a Ladder League

1. From **My Leagues**, click **New League**.
2. **Step 1 — Configure:**
   - Enter a league name.
   - Choose your sport.
   - Select **Singles** or **Doubles** (Doubles challenges are not yet available).
   - Select **Ladder** as the mode.
   - Set your match format and time limit.
   - Set the **challenge window** — how many spots above themselves a player may challenge.
   - Click **Next**.
3. **Step 2 — Add Players:**
   - Add all players with names and ratings. Seeding order is by rating (highest first).
   - No schedule is generated. Matches are created when players issue and accept challenges.
   - Click **Launch League** when ready.
4. Confirm the launch.
5. Share player codes (see Section 7).

---

## 6. Adding and Managing Players

- Inside a league dashboard, open the **Players** panel from the header.
- You can view all player codes and copy invite messages for each player from that panel.
- Player ratings influence seeding at launch but do not update automatically after launch.

---

## 7. Sharing Player Access Codes

Each player receives a unique **access code** that is their key to the league.

1. After launching, the codes screen displays immediately.
2. You can return to codes at any time: open the league → header → **Players**.
3. For each player, copy their **invite message** — it includes the production URL and their code in one message.
4. Send it directly to each player via text, email, or your group chat.
5. **Do not post all codes in a public group.** Each code is personal.
6. If a player loses their code, you can retrieve it from the Players panel and resend it.

---

## 8. Organizer Score Entry (Official Score)

You may enter an official score for any match regardless of player submission.

1. Open the league dashboard → **Schedule** tab.
2. Find the match and click **Record Official Score**.
3. Fill in the score and submit.
4. Official scores entered by the organizer take effect immediately without requiring player confirmation.

---

## 9. Player Score Submission and Confirmation

Players may submit their own scores:

1. A player opens the league, finds the match, and submits the score.
2. The opponent receives a notification (visible in the bell icon at the top).
3. The opponent opens the match and clicks **Confirm** or **Dispute**.
4. **Confirm** — the score is recorded.
5. **Dispute** — the match is flagged. As organizer, you will see the dispute and can enter the official score to resolve it.

---

## 10. Ladder Challenges

**Issuing a challenge (organizer on behalf of players):**

1. Open the league dashboard → **Challenges** tab.
2. Click **Issue Challenge**.
3. Select the challenger and the challenged player (must be within the challenge window above the challenger).
4. Confirm.

**Players issue challenges themselves** from their own view — see the Player Guide.

**Accepting or declining:**

1. The challenged player sees a notification.
2. They open the challenge and click **Accept** or **Decline**.
3. If accepted, a match appears in the schedule.

**After the match is played:**

- Enter the score the same way as any other match (see Section 8).
- Ladder positions update automatically based on match results.

---

## 11. Ending a League

When competition is complete:

1. Open the league → header menu → **End League**.
2. Confirm the action.
3. The league status changes to **Ended**. All data is preserved but the league becomes read-only. No new scores, challenges, or edits are possible.

---

## 12. Archiving and Restoring a League

**Archive** moves a league to a separate section of My Leagues for tidiness. It does not delete anything.

1. On My Leagues, find an **Ended** league and click **Archive**.
2. To see archived leagues, toggle the archive view in My Leagues.
3. To restore, click **Restore** on the archived league. It returns to the main list with Ended status.

---

## 13. Duplicating as a New Season

To start a fresh season with the same players and settings:

1. On My Leagues, find the league and click **Duplicate**.
2. Enter a new league name and confirm.
3. A new league is created with the same players, settings, and seeding — but no match history.
4. Share new player codes for the duplicated league (codes are unique per league).

---

## 14. Permanently Deleting a League

Deletion is **permanent and irreversible**. All match history, standings, and codes are lost.

1. Duplicate the league first if you want to preserve the player roster for a future season.
2. On My Leagues, click the delete action for the league.
3. Type the league name to confirm.
4. Click **Delete**. There is no undo.

---

## 15. What "Ended" and "Archived" Mean

| Status       | What it means                                                               |
| ------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Active**   | League is in progress. All actions available.                               |
| **Ended**    | Season is over. Everything is read-only. Data is preserved for reference.   |
| **Archived** | Ended and moved out of the main list. Still read-only and fully accessible. |

---

## 16. Mobile Use

The app works in any modern mobile browser.

1. Open the production URL in Safari (iPhone) or Chrome (Android).
2. Tap **Add to Home Screen** for quick access (optional).
3. All features — score entry, challenges, standings — work on mobile.
4. If the layout appears zoomed or distorted, try refreshing the page.

---

## 17. Reporting Problems

If something does not work as expected:

1. Note the league name, the action you were taking, and what happened.
2. Take a screenshot if possible.
3. Report the issue using the feedback log or contact the app operator directly.
4. Do not delete any data to work around a problem — document it first and wait for guidance.
